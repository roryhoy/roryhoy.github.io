Uncanny: A Telematic nO(t)pera

 · 2016

A live telematic performance created by Doug Van Nort and hosted in the DisPerSion Lab at York University, featuring:
  • Chris Chafe from CCRMA at Stanford University
  • Pauline Oliveros, Jonas Braasch, and Zach Layton from CRAIVE at Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute
  • Doug Van Nort in the DisPerSion Lab at York University
  • Anne Bourne in the Special Projects Gallery at York University

Playing with presence through lifesize projection and suspended materials, Uncanny was presented as a blended stage of live and telematically visualized performers.

Responsible for network mapping design, I created a web based interface for voting interaction in which viewers could instruct performers based on live input. The voting page was hosted on the DisPerSion website and allowed the audience to vote for timbral and speed changes, and which performer should be playing at that time. The interface was constructed using NexusUI, a web audio interface library for HTML and JS.

Votes were calculated and sent to a screen each performer could see, allowing them to adjust their playing according to data provided by viewers which was updated every minute.
